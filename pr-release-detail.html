The Saving Grace for Pandemic-Fueled Business Closures: Online Streaming

The pandemic hit businesses hard, forcing a nearly 60% of permanent business closures after the dust settled towards the end of 2020. Many of these businesses were not prepared for the need to pivot digitally, relying on technology to get their products and services out to their clientele. For those that were unwilling or not able to keep up with the pace of innovation in such a short amount of time, they faded into the background – that is, until they discovered the power and potential of streaming as a ‘side hustle.’

For the businesses that relied on in-person connections and foot traffic to turn a profit, the pandemic shocked them: they could no longer do business the way they had for the last 200-years. It was a lot to stomach in a short amount of time, but thankfully, the cutting-edge technology at our fingertips reminded us that we had alternative options.

Technology to the Rescue

While businesses set up online stores, opened social media profiles, and struggled to learn about building email lists, something much easier and simpler fell at their footsteps: live streaming. See, the same problem that hit businesses also hit the consumers told they could no longer shop in person or go to a local movie theater. They, too, were searching for a way to still be consumers and remain entertained from their living rooms.

The answer for both parties became increasingly obvious in video streaming, where businesses (content creators) could create an experience and answer questions, much like they had in person. This real-time, live filming recreated that person-to-person experience that so many everyday people were missing. If businesses we able to recreate that intimacy, even if only for a moment, viewers were hooked.

But, big tech companies and social media sites were hip to the shift, which is why content creators then – and now – are still paying a price for their content in the form of fees, regulations, and so forth.

Returning Content Ownership to You

That’s what inspired us here at Streaming Plug to do something about it. We see the future of streaming to be integral to how every business and brand is able to interact with its customers. To us, these businesses should be compensated for the content they are feverishly providing to online viewers, which is why Streaming Plug is a social media site that doesn’t take the fees. It’s a 100% commission-based ownership arrangement that rewards businesses for being innovative.

Here are 3 reasons why as a business owner, you should want to own your streaming content:

  • Your streamed content is, many times, the only thing that is making consumers aware of your business offerings.
  • Your streamed content is 100% something that you have worked hard to produce. You shouldn’t have to pay a fee on it.
  • Streaming is one of the most effective ways to garner massive exposure to an audience of one-billion people or more.

We’re merely that connective piece. Don’t let your content go to waste on other social media platforms. Join Streaming Plug today.
